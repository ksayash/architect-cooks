Curd Rice

What makes this dish special

Curd rice is a quick and easy dish that is perfect for a light meal or snack. It is made with cooked rice, yogurt, and a variety of spices. This dish is known for its digestive properties, making it a great choice for those with sensitive stomachs. The combination of rice and yogurt provides a cooling and soothing effect, especially during hot weather. This recipe uses leftover rice, making it a great way to reduce food waste. The tempering adds a burst of flavor and aroma, enhancing the overall taste of the dish. It is a versatile dish that can be customized to suit individual preferences.

Recipe

Ingredients: - Cooked rice: 1 cup - Curd (yogurt): ½ cup - Salt to taste

For tempering: - Oil: 1 Tbsp - Black mustard seeds: ½ tsp - Urad daal (split black lentils): 1 tsp - Curry leaves: 5-6, chopped - Ginger: ½ tsp, grated - Green chilies: 1-2, finely chopped - Red chili powder: ¼ tsp - Hing (asafoetida): a pinch

Instructions: 1. In a bowl, mix together the rice, curd, and salt. Ensure the rice is cold for the best texture. 2. Set aside the rice mixture. 3. In a pan, heat oil. Add the mustard seeds and daal. 4. Once the daal turns golden, add the curry leaves, ginger, hing, and green chilies. 5. Turn off the flame, add the red chili powder, and pour the tempering over the rice. 6. Serve chilled.

Tips: - If using freshly cooked rice, allow it to cool for a few hours before mixing with the curd. - Adjust the amount of red chili powder according to your spice preference. - For a creamier texture, add a little milk to the curd before mixing with the rice.

Architecture Tip

"Normally what happens is that we see that the balconies, we have a great desire to put green plants, but the balcony's size goes away. So a very easy way is that the balcony, many times, is your iron railings. So on those railings, you weld some rings, and in those rings, you put your potted plants, which are hanging outside the balcony." As a result, you get greenery, your balcony space isn't reduced, and the railings that allow for a disturbance of privacy are covered by the plants.
