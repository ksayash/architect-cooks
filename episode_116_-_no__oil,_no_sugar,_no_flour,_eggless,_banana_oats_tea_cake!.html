Banana Oat Tea Cake

What makes this dish special

This is a simple and healthy dessert that is perfect for any time of day. It's made with just a few ingredients, and it's naturally sweetened with bananas. This recipe is a great way to use up ripe bananas, and it's a healthy alternative to traditional cakes. It's also a great option for those who are looking for a gluten-free or vegan dessert. The cake is moist and fluffy, and it has a delicious banana flavor. The addition of cinnamon and vanilla extract adds a touch of warmth and sweetness. You can also add chocolate chips for an extra touch of indulgence.

Recipe

Ingredients: * Banana, ripe: ½ cup (pureed in blender) * Rolled oats: ½ cup * Cinnamon powder: ¼ tsp * Vanilla essence: ¼ tsp * Chocolate chips: 1 tsp (optional) * Baking soda: ¼ tsp

Instructions: 1. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celsius on bake mode (bottom heat only). 2. Mix all the ingredients well. 3. Pour into a pre-greased small baking dish. 4. Decorate with some chocolate chips if desired. 5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean. 6. Let cool for 10 minutes before removing from the baking dish. 7. Serve hot as a perfect healthy breakfast dish, with fresh-cut fruits to make it even more healthy.

Tips: * Rolled oats give the best results, but you can also use instant oats. * If you want to make this cake vegan, use vegan chocolate chips. * Let the cake cool for 10 minutes before removing it from the baking dish to prevent it from crumbling.

Architecture Tip

Doors have hinges to support the door from one side. If your door is taller or heavier, the normal three hinges may not be enough to take the load. In that case, you have to increase the number of hinges so that the door doesn't become lopsided because of the weight. The top of the door should have hinges that are closer together.
