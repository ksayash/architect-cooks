Kaanji and Chai Masala

What makes this dish special

Kaanji is a popular drink in Punjab, India, especially in the winter months. It is known for its tangy flavor and beautiful color. Traditionally, it is made with black carrots, which are only available for a short time. This recipe uses red carrots and beetroot as a substitute. The chai masala is a blend of spices that are known for their immunity-boosting properties. It is a perfect way to warm up on a cold day. The spices can be adjusted to your liking. The chai masala is a great addition to any tea lover's collection.

Recipe

Kaanji Ingredients: * 1 medium red carrot, cut into small cubes * 1 medium beetroot, cut into small cubes * 2 Tbsp salt, or to taste * 2 Tbsp Banarsi rai (red mustard), ground finely * 2 liters water

Kaanji Instructions: 1. Add all ingredients together in a glass or ceramic container. 2. Mix well. 3. Pour into bottles. 4. Shake at least two times per day. 5. Ferment for three days.

Chai Masala Ingredients: * 2 Tbsp green cardamom, with skin on * 1 Tbsp cloves * 2 Tbsp cinnamon * 2 Tbsp black peppercorns * 2 Tbsp fennel seeds, lightly roasted * 2 Tbsp ginger powder

Chai Masala Instructions: 1. Grind all ingredients together finely. 2. Add a teaspoon to one cup of water. 3. Boil for 3-4 minutes before making tea.

Architecture Tip

When designing a house or a flat, it is important to provide at least two to three call bell ringers in all the crucial places in the house. One should be in the central lobby or foyer, or the dining area. Another should be in a bedroom where you spend a lot of time, or on the terrace. This is essential, otherwise the call bell may not be heard.
