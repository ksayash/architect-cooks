Semolina Cake

What makes this dish special

This semolina cake, also known as Sambali or Revani, is a Middle Eastern dessert that is easy to make and low in fat. This recipe is a Turkish variation of the Egyptian Basbousa. It is a quick and easy dessert that is perfect for any occasion. The cake is made with semolina, milk, sugar, and baking soda, and is garnished with almonds. The cake is baked until golden brown, and then soaked in a simple syrup made with sugar, water, rose water, and lemon juice. The cake is best served after it has soaked in the syrup for at least 4 to 5 hours.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fine semolina
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • Almonds for garnish
  • Oil for greasing

Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp rose water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 170 degrees Celsius.
  2. Grease a baking dish of suitable size.
  3. In a bowl, mix together the semolina, sugar, milk, and baking soda.
  4. Pour the semolina mixture into the greased baking dish.
  5. Pat to smooth the top.
  6. Cover and set aside for an hour.
  7. In the meantime, in a pan, boil together sugar and water for the syrup.
  8. Once it comes to a boil, add the rose water and lemon juice and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  9. Switch off the gas and let it cool.
  10. After an hour, make a pattern in the semolina mixture.
  11. Decorate with almonds.
  12. Bake for about 40 minutes, until golden brown.
  13. Take out, cut along the pattern made earlier, and pour cool sugar syrup over the hot cake.
  14. Rest for at least 4 to 5 hours before serving.

Architecture Tip

"The material to be used outside the house in the paving areas." It is important to not use impervious material, but rather porous material. This is so that "the surface water can percolate and rejuvenate the ground water."
