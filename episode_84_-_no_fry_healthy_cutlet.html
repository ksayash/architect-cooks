Bread and Potato Cutlets

What makes this dish special

These bread and potato cutlets are a healthy alternative to traditional cutlets, which are often deep-fried. This recipe uses bread and potatoes as the base, and the cutlets are shallow-fried in a pan, minimizing the amount of oil used. The recipe is a great option for those who are health-conscious but still want to enjoy a tasty snack. The cutlets are also versatile and can be served as an evening snack or a morning breakfast. The recipe is easy to customize to your liking by adjusting the spices and adding other vegetables.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 potato (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 onion (finely chopped)
  • Green coriander
  • Green chilies
  • Amchoor
  • Salt
  • Grated ginger
  • RCP
  • Coriander powder
  • 2 slices of bread (white or brown)
  • Oil for shallow frying
  • Water in a flat plate

Instructions:

  1. Mix well all ingredients for the filling. Keep aside.
  2. Soak the bread slice gently in water and press flat between palms to squeeze the water.
  3. Put some mixture in the center and roll while pressing to form a ball. Now give the desired shape for the cutlet.
  4. Once all the cutlets are shaped, put a shallow pan on the flame.
  5. Grease with 1 tbsp of oil.
  6. Once hot, put the cutlets.
  7. Shallow fry them on both sides till crispy brown.
  8. Serve hot with ketchup or green chutney.

Cooking tip:

  • Drain the potatoes after boiling to prevent them from becoming too wet.
  • Be careful not to overfill the bread with the potato mixture, as this can make the cutlets difficult to shape.
  • Do not move the cutlets immediately after placing them in the pan. Allow the bread to cook a little before flipping.
