Whole Wheat Coffee Bean Cookies

What makes this dish special

These cookies are a delicious and healthier twist on traditional treats, using whole wheat flour for a nutty flavor and added fiber. The cookies are flavored with coffee and cocoa powder, creating a rich, aromatic experience that's perfect for coffee lovers. Shaped like coffee beans, they are a delightful treat to serve with your morning or evening coffee. The recipe is quick and easy, requiring only a few simple ingredients and minimal baking time. These cookies are not overly sweet, allowing the coffee flavor to shine through. They are a great option for those looking for a healthier, yet satisfying, snack.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 40g soft butter
  • 2 Tbsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp instant coffee powder
  • 1 Tbsp lukewarm water
  • ¼ cup + 2 Tbsp whole wheat flour
  • 1 Tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1 Tbsp cornflour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celsius on bake mode.
  2. Mix the coffee powder in lukewarm water and keep aside.
  3. Whisk the sugar and butter together until soft and fluffy.
  4. Add all the other ingredients and mix well. If the dough is too dry, add ½ Tbsp of water. If the dough is too wet, add ½ Tbsp of whole wheat flour.
  5. Bring the dough together with your hand. Do not knead.
  6. Shape into ovals of desired size and make a cut on top with a butter knife.
  7. Bake for 10 minutes.
  8. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before serving.

Architecture Tip

During the construction of any house, a common problem is seepage in external walls or on the toilet walls. Of course, we have to be very careful with the type of pipes we use, and they shouldn't burst, so the quality should be good. But one more thing that you can do is that when external walls of the house or toilet walls, or wherever wet areas are, when you do brickwork, in the brickwork's mortar, you should mix in some waterproofing compound. If you do that, it will solve the water seepage problem.
