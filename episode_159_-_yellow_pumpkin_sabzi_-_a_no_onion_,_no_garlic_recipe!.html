Pumpkin Sabzi

What makes this dish special

This pumpkin sabzi is a delicious and flavorful dish that is perfect for any occasion. What makes this dish special is that it is made without onion or garlic, making it perfect for those who are fasting or who are looking for a lighter meal. The dish is also made with a variety of spices, which gives it a unique and complex flavor. The addition of jaggery or sugar adds a touch of sweetness, while the amchur powder provides a tangy counterpoint. The dish is also very healthy, as it is made with pumpkin, which is a good source of vitamins and minerals. This dish is sure to be a hit with everyone who tries it.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • Yellow Pumpkin (Kaddu) : ½ kg (cut into cubes)
  • Tomatoes : 2 medium (coarsely chopped)
  • 2 Tbsp oil (preferably mustard)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp panchforan
  • 2 green chili (slit)
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ + ¼ cup water
  • 1 tsp jaggery or sugar
  • 1 tsp amchur / dry mango powder
  • 2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

Instructions:

  1. In a large kadai/pan, heat oil and saute bay leaf, panchforan, green chili and ginger until aromatic.
  2. Now add the pumpkin and tomatoes and saute for 2-3 minutes. Add powdered spices except amchoor.
  3. Saute for 2 minutes until the spices are well combined.
  4. Add ½ cup water, cover and cook on medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring in between.
  5. Cook until the pumpkin is soft, adding more water if required.
  6. Now add the jaggery/sugar, amchur and another ¼ cup water. Mix well and cook for another couple of minutes on low flame with lid on. Adjust consistency as required by adding water.
  7. Garnish with chopped coriander and serve hot.

Tip: If you want to make this dish quickly, simply pressure cook it for one whistle and then simmer for 3-4 minutes.
