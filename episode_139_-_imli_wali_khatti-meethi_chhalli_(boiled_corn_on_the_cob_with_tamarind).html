Chatpati Imli Wali Masaledar Challi

What makes this dish special

This recipe is a traditional, guilt-free snack that is perfect for the rainy season. It is a chatpata, or spicy, snack that is sure to please. The recipe uses corn on the cob, also known as bhutta or challi, and is flavored with tamarind pulp, sweet imli chutney, and spices. The American sweet corn is recommended because it is a little sweet in taste and very soft. This makes it flavorful and easy to eat. This recipe is a great way to enjoy a delicious and healthy snack without any guilt.

Recipe

Ingredients: * American corn on the cob: 1 (cut into half) * Tamarind pulp: 2 Tbsp * Water: ½ cup * Kashmiri red chili powder: ½ tsp * Black salt: ½ tsp * Sweet Imli chutney: 2 Tbsp * Wooden skewers: 2 nos. (for serving)

Instructions: 1. Put all the ingredients except the tamarind chutney in a pressure cooker. 2. Cook on high until the whistle blows, then on low for another 7-8 minutes. If the corn used is not of the American sweet corn variety, cook for a little longer, until soft. 3. Use skewers to help handle it better. 4. Serve hot in a deep bowl, with the tamarind water and tamarind chutney poured over it.
