Vrat-Friendly Green Chutney

What makes this dish special

Indian food is often accompanied by chutney, and green chutney is a staple. However, many green chutney recipes include onion and garlic, which are not allowed during fasting or vrat. This recipe is a vrat-friendly version of green chutney, so you can enjoy it even when you're fasting. It's a simple, quick, and delicious recipe that is perfect for those who are looking for a vrat-friendly chutney. The chutney is made with green coriander, mint leaves, green chilies, lemon juice, salt, kala namak, and roasted jeera. It's a refreshing and flavorful chutney that can be enjoyed with a variety of dishes.

Recipe

Ingredients: * 1 cup green coriander * ¼ cup mint leaves * 3-4 green chilies * 2 Tbsp lemon juice * Salt to taste * ¼ tsp kala namak * ½ tsp roasted jeera * Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Roughly chop the green coriander.
  2. Halve the green chilies.
  3. Grind all ingredients to a coarse paste in a blender, adding water if required.
  4. Serve chilled.

Cooking Tip: * Add water a little at a time to avoid making the chutney too thin.

Architecture Tip

Many homes have water tanks, whether they are sintex or concrete. It's important to regularly clean the water tank. There should be a water outlet at the base of the water tank so that when you need to clean it, you can open the outlet and the water will drain out. This should be done at least once every six months.
