Sautéed Vegetables

What makes this dish special

This dish is a quick and easy way to get a healthy dose of vegetables. It's a colorful and flavorful dish that's perfect for a light meal or a side dish. The combination of different vegetables provides a variety of nutrients and textures. The garlic and lemon juice add a bright and zesty flavor. This dish is especially suitable for winter months, but can be enjoyed any time of the year. The vegetables are cooked until they are tender-crisp, so they retain their nutrients and have a satisfying crunch. It's a great way to use up leftover vegetables in your refrigerator. This recipe is a favorite of adults and children alike.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 medium onion, cut into petals
  • 1 medium potato, boiled and cubed
  • ½ cup green beans, cut into long strips
  • 1 cup mushrooms, quartered
  • 1 cup carrots, cut into long strips
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp finely chopped coriander

Instructions:

  1. Steam or microwave the broccoli, cauliflower, green beans, and carrots to par-cook.
  2. In a pan, heat the butter and oil. Add the garlic and fry until light pink.
  3. Add the onion and fry until translucent.
  4. Add the potatoes and mushrooms. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add all the other vegetables, salt, and pepper. Sauté on high flame for 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Add the lemon juice and coriander. Mix well and serve hot.

Tips:

  • For best results, use fresh, high-quality vegetables.
  • Don't overcook the vegetables, as they will become mushy.
  • Adjust the amount of salt and pepper to your liking.
  • Serve with a bowl of soup and a side of garlic toast or normal toast.
