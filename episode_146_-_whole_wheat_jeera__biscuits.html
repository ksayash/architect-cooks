Jeera Atta Biscuits

What makes this dish special

These Jeera Atta Biscuits are a delightful treat that combines the subtle sweetness of caster sugar with the earthy, savory notes of roasted cumin (jeera). Made with whole wheat flour, these biscuits offer a healthier alternative to traditional cookies, making them a guilt-free indulgence. The addition of ghee lends a rich, buttery flavor, while a hint of salt enhances the overall taste profile. These biscuits are perfect for enjoying with a cup of tea or coffee, or even on their own as a light snack. The simple yet satisfying recipe ensures a quick and easy baking experience, resulting in a batch of crispy, flavorful cookies that are sure to please.

Recipe

Ingredients: * Whole wheat flour: ½ cup (63g) * Ghee: 2 Tbsp (28g) * Caster sugar: 3 Tbsp (33g) * Baking powder: ¼ tsp * Roasted Jeera: 1 tsp * Salt: ¼ tsp * Milk: 1½ - 2 Tbsp

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add the ghee and caster sugar. Whisk for a minute or two until fluffy.
  2. Add the flour, salt, and jeera. Mix well with a fork.
  3. Knead gently with your hand to bring the dough together.
  4. Preheat the OTG to 170 degrees Celsius.
  5. Shape the dough into cookies and make a pattern with a fork.
  6. Bake for 15-18 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Let cool for 15-20 minutes before serving.

Tips:

  • Rotate the baking sheet by 180 degrees halfway through baking to ensure even cooking.
  • Wait for the cookies to cool completely before serving, as they will be soggy and crumbly when warm.

Architecture Tip

Buildings are normally they go vertically high, but due to the height restrictions and people want to get the maximum floor area ratio, that is the maximum permissible built-up area according to the local bylaws, so we don't give height very much to floor to floor. As a result, at times the ceiling tends to be a little too low. For that, a trick is that whatever ceiling you paint, do not use a dark color for the ceiling. Ideally, paint it white. If you don't want white, then any color of the lightest possible shade. That way, visually, you'll get the ceiling a little high.
