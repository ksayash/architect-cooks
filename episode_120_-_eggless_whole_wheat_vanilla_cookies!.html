Whole Wheat Vanilla Cookies

What makes this dish special

These whole wheat vanilla cookies are a healthy and delicious treat that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. They are made with whole wheat flour, which is a good source of fiber, and ghee, which is a healthy fat. The cookies are also eggless, making them a great option for people with egg allergies. The vanilla essence adds a touch of sweetness and flavor, making these cookies irresistible. They are perfect for a quick snack, a dessert, or even a breakfast treat. These cookies are easy to make and can be ready in just 30 minutes. They are also a great way to use up leftover whole wheat flour.

Recipe

Ingredients: * ½ cup whole wheat flour * ¼ cup caster sugar * ¼ cup ghee (semi-melted) * ¼ tsp vanilla essence * Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celsius on bake mode (bottom heating only).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the caster sugar and semi-melted ghee for about a minute until the color lightens.
  3. Add the vanilla essence and a pinch of salt, mixing well.
  4. Gradually add the whole wheat flour and mix until a soft dough forms.
  5. Scoop out small portions of the dough and shape them into round cookies.
  6. Place the cookies on a baking tray, leaving some space between each.
  7. Bake for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the base is golden brown. Avoid overbaking.
  8. Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool completely on the tray before serving. This allows them to crisp up.

Architecture Tip

When building a house, it is important to consider the addition of a veranda or balcony. People like to have greens all around them, whether it's pots or hanging plants. A point that is often missed during construction is giving off a water point. Water points are necessary so that watering the plants is not an issue.
