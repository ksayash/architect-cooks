Singhade ke Atte ki Puri

What makes this dish special

This recipe is a delicious and easy-to-make puri made with Singhade ka Atta (water chestnut flour). It's a great option for those observing fasts, as it's a vrat-friendly dish. The addition of mashed potatoes helps bind the dough, making it easier to roll out. The puris are deep-fried until golden brown and crispy, making them a satisfying and flavorful treat. You can use Kuttu ka Atta instead of Singhade ka Atta, if you prefer. The procedure remains the same. If you are making paratha, it will soak up more oil.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup Singhade ka Atta
  • 1/4 cup Boiled potatoes (peeled and mashed)
  • 2 tsp Oil
  • Pinch of Salt (optional)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the Singhade ka Atta, mashed potatoes, oil, and salt (if using).
  2. Mix well with a spatula.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough. Be careful not to add too much water, as the dough can become sticky.
  4. Heat oil in a kadhai or deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Grease your hands with a little oil.
  6. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into balls.
  7. Place a dough ball between two plastic sheets.
  8. Roll out the dough into a small puri.
  9. Gently remove the top plastic sheet.
  10. Carefully transfer the puri to your hand, then remove the bottom plastic sheet.
  11. Gently slide the puri into the hot oil.
  12. Fry until golden brown and puffed up.
  13. Remove from oil and drain on a paper towel.
  14. Serve hot.

Architecture Tip

When you are painting your house, use the best quality paint that is available in the market. Although it may seem a little expensive at the time, if you use a lower quality paint, you will have to repaint every two to three years. If you use a better quality paint, it will last for at least five to six years. The money is much better spent by investing in a better quality paint.
