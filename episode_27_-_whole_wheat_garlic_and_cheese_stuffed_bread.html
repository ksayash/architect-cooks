Whole Wheat Stuffed Garlic Bread

What makes this dish special

This recipe is a healthy version of a classic comfort food. It's made with whole wheat flour instead of all-purpose flour, making it a more nutritious option. The bread is stuffed with a flavorful mixture of cheese, capsicum, and spices, creating a delicious and satisfying snack or meal. The addition of garlic butter and sesame seeds on top adds a touch of elegance and enhances the overall taste. This recipe is perfect for kids and adults alike, and it can be customized to suit individual preferences by adding different vegetables or spices. It's a great way to enjoy a cheesy, garlicky treat without feeling guilty. This recipe is also yeast-free, making it a quick and easy option for those who don't want to wait for dough to rise.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 3 tsp crushed garlic
  • 1 Tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp sour curd
  • 4-5 Tbsp warm milk
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • 3 Tbsp soft butter
  • Chili garlic flakes to taste
  • Mixed herbs as required
  • 2 Tbsp finely chopped capsicum
  • 2 Tbsp sweet corn (optional)
  • 2 Tbsp grated processed cheese
  • ½ cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1 Tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 Tbsp finely chopped coriander

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 170 degrees Celsius.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the flour, sugar, salt, baking powder, and baking soda. Add half the garlic, some sesame seeds, mixed herbs, and chili flakes.
  3. Add the curd, oil, and 3 Tbsp of milk. Bring together with a ladle.
  4. Roll into a ball, knead for about 45 seconds (add more milk if required), and cover with a damp cloth for at least 20 minutes.
  5. Mix the butter with the rest of the garlic, mixed herbs, and red chili flakes. Add some coriander leaves.
  6. Grease a baking plate with the butter mixture.
  7. Knead the dough for 30 seconds. Apply some flour and roll into a 9-inch circle. Place this on the baking plate.
  8. Brush the surface with butter mixture, leaving about half an inch empty along the edges.
  9. On one half, put the cheese, capsicum, salt, coriander, herbs, and chili flakes.
  10. Fold into half and seal the edges with water. Make a pattern with a fork.
  11. Brush some butter mixture on top and sprinkle sesame seeds. Make cuts on the top layer, as shown in the video.
  12. Bake for 15 to 25 minutes, until golden brown.
  13. Take out from the oven, brush with remaining butter, cut along the lines, and serve hot.

Architecture Tip

"Whosoever stays in the topmost floor of any house or apartment or a bungalow, you are well aware that the most upper floor is because the ceiling is exposed to direct, the terrace is exposed to direct sunlight, so it tends to get very warm. It is at least three to four degrees warmer in temperature than the lower floors. So to sort that, when we do terrace treat, we do not directly concrete on the slab that is cast. You should always give a buffer inside."

"There are a lot of materials available in the market these days. You can take any expert's advice. One such material that's coming to mind is the lightweight cement concrete blocks. So the lightweight concrete blocks is that your load will also not add on the roof. You can give it a layer. That kind of acts as a buffer insulation zone, and on top of that, then you can do your normal flooring."
