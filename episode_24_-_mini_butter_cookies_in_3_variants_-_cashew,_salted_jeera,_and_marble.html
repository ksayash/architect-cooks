Mini Butter Cookies

What makes this dish special

These mini butter cookies are a quick and easy treat that can be made with ingredients you probably already have at home. The recipe is simple and doesn't require any special equipment, making it perfect for a last-minute dessert or snack. What sets these cookies apart is the variety of flavors you can create with just one basic dough. The recipe includes three flavor variations: salted jeera, cashew, and marble. The mini size makes them perfect for enjoying a variety of flavors in one sitting. These cookies are so delicious that you'll never want to buy store-bought butter cookies again.

Recipe

Ingredients: * 50g soft butter * 65g (1/2 cup) all-purpose flour * 25g (3 Tbsp) caster sugar * 1/2 tsp roasted cumin seeds * Pinch of salt * 1 tsp cocoa powder * 1 tsp coarsely ground cashews

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add the butter and sugar. Whisk for a minute, until fluffy.
  2. Add the flour. Mix well with a fork until it comes together. Use your hands to bind the dough (do not knead).
  3. Divide the dough into 3 parts.
  4. In the first part, add jeera and salt. Mix well. Shape into a cuboid, wrap it in a piece of butter paper, and put aside.
  5. In the second part, add the cashews and repeat the process.
  6. Divide the third part into two. Add the cocoa powder to one part and mix. Now shape both parts together into a cube as directed in the video.
  7. Put the 3 doughs in the fridge for about 10 to 15 minutes.
  8. Preheat the oven on bake mode to 170 degrees Celsius.
  9. Cut the cookies into the desired shape and bake for 10 to 12 minutes.
  10. Serve once cool and crunchy.

Tips: * Do not overcook the cookies. The base should be lightly browned, and the top should remain whitish. * If the base of the cookies starts to brown too quickly, transfer them to another plate to prevent overcooking. * Let the cookies cool completely before serving to allow them to become crunchy.
